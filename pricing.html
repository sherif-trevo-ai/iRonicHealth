The Pricing Philosophy

Revenue That Proves
Value First

Most B2B SaaS companies charge a platform fee and hope the customer sees value. We do the opposite — we prove value first, then charge. This is not a marketing tactic. It's the only model that makes sense when your AI has to earn trust in Egypt's healthcare market.

The Principle
"An insurer CFO does not need to approve a budget to start our pilot. The pilot pays for itself from Day 1 — and we only invoice against what we recover. That's not a sales pitch. That's a legal contract."
— Eng. Sherif Shawqi, Chairman & CEO, iRonic Health
💯
Zero Budget Approval Required
The pilot starts at zero cost to the insurer. No procurement cycle, no CFO sign-off on a budget line, no quarterly approval wait. The first invoice arrives only when fraud is validated — and it's paid from the savings we generated.
Result: 6–8 week pilot close vs 6–12 month traditional SaaS sale
⚖️
Aligned Incentives by Design
We earn 25% of validated fraud savings. If we detect $1M in fraud, the insurer keeps $750K and we earn $250K. If we detect nothing, we earn nothing. This forces us to train better models, be honest about false positives, and focus on real recoveries — not just impressive dashboards.
Incentive alignment: we only profit from actual fraud, not claimed fraud
📈
PMPM Transition: Value Already Proven
After a 90-day pilot with documented ROI, the commercial conversation shifts: "We've shown you a 4% loss ratio improvement. The PMPM subscription costs $0.75 per member per month. At 500K lives, that's $375K/year — and we've already shown you $1M+ in annual savings." The sale writes itself.
PMPM transition happens only after documented ROI — never before
