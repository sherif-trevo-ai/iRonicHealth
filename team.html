How It Started

From a Paper Problem
to an AI Platform

The venture didn't start with a product idea. It started with a diagnosis — Egypt's private health insurance system is operationally primitive in a way that costs the entire ecosystem billions of pounds a year.

💡
The observation
90%+ of Claims on Paper
Sherif and Islam found the same statistic independently — confirmed by Yodawy's CEO in TechCrunch (Feb 2023): "90% of Egypt's prescriptions and insurance claims are recorded on paper." No AI. No real-time verification. A $373M market running on fax and phone calls.
⚖️
The trigger · April 2026
FRA Licensed 6 TPAs
Egypt's Financial Regulatory Authority issued temporary licences to six healthcare TPAs under the Unified Insurance Law 155/2024. This opened a regulatory channel that didn't exist before — and created an 18–36 month first-mover window before incumbents could mobilise.
🏗️
The decision
From Healio to iRonic Health
The venture began as Healio (HealthLink AI). As the scope crystallised — connecting all 8 parties in the ecosystem, not just automating claims — the brand evolved into iRonic Health, operating as the healthcare arm of the legally registered Ironic Group.
🚀
Now · Seed Round Open
$1M to Prove the Thesis
Registered entity. Brand trademarked. Architecture designed. GlobeMed engagement in progress. Now raising $1M to build the fraud models, integrate the first TPA, and document the first 90-day pilot ROI that no Egyptian insurer has ever seen before.
"Egypt's private health insurance market runs on paper. Every step in the chain — from prescription to pharmacy to insurer — involves manual entry, a fax, or a phone call. That's not an inefficiency. That's an opportunity."
— Eng. Sherif Shawqi, Chairman & CEO, iRonic Health
🔍
The problem is documented, not hypothetical
RGA Middle East's survey of 37 insurers confirms 2–10% fraud rates. Assiut University research confirms antibiotic over-prescription. Uppsala Reports document Unictam counterfeiting. The data exists — no Egyptian insurer acts on it in real time.
⏰
The timing is specific, not generic
Law 155/2024 + FRA's April 2026 TPA licencing + rising loss ratios (50–60% YoY) + zero AI incumbents = a window that is specific, measurable, and temporary. We move now or watch it close.
🤝
The team is complementary, not redundant
Business strategy + clinical governance. GTM execution + FRA compliance. Investor relations + Medical Syndicate credibility. The two founders cover every stakeholder dimension of an insurer sale — CMO, CFO, and CIO concerns.

What Drives Us

Four Principles We
Build Every Decision Around

These are not wall posters. They are the specific choices we made when designing the platform, the pricing model, and the compliance architecture.

01 · Principle
🇪🇬
Egypt-Native First
We didn't adapt a global model to Egypt — we built from Egyptian clinical data, ICD/CPT codes, pharmacy patterns, and documented fraud cases. Unictam counterfeiting, antibiotic over-prescription, ICU billing games — these are Egypt-specific problems that need Egypt-specific AI. A model trained on US Medicare patterns would catch less than 30% of Egypt's fraud archetypes.
25–40 Egypt-specific archetypes · No global model has these
02 · Principle
⚖️
Governance Over Speed
Every high-impact AI decision has a licensed CMO from the Egyptian Medical Syndicate as a required step. We chose this not because FRA forced us to — but because it's the right architecture. An AI that fires autonomously in healthcare creates liability, distrust, and false positives. An AI that supports a named physician's decision creates accountability, auditability, and insurer trust.
CMO sign-off on every denial · FRA-compliant from day one
03 · Principle
🤝
Value Before Revenue
We earn 25% of validated fraud savings in the pilot — nothing until we prove the value. This isn't just a sales tactic. It's a philosophical commitment: we don't deserve to be paid until we've earned it. This contingent model eliminates every financial objection, aligns our incentives perfectly with the insurer's, and means every EGP we recover is genuinely proven, not projected.
Zero upfront cost · 25% of validated savings only
04 · Principle
🔦
Transparent Intelligence
Every AI decision has a reason — displayed to the CMO, logged for the FRA, visible to the insurer. We built the platform to explain itself: which archetype matched, at what confidence, with what evidence. No black boxes in healthcare. An AI that can't explain its own reasoning is an AI that can't be trusted — and an insurer that can't explain their denial to a member has a compliance problem.
Full FRA audit trail · Human-readable AI reasoning

Why "Not an Insurer" Matters
As a Decision-Support Software Provider (SaaS), iRonic Health does not require an FRA insurance licence — enabling faster market entry, simplified procurement, and no regulatory conflict with insurer clients. The platform sells software, not insurance. This distinction took legal counsel to architect correctly — and creates a moat for any competitor who builds the wrong entity type.
